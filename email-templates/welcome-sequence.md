## Email 2: Quick Win Tip (Day 2)

**Subject:** The #1 grow light mistake (and how to avoid it)

**Preview Text:** Don't trust "wattage equivalent" claims - here's what to look for instead

**Body:**

Hey,

Hope the checklist is helpful! Today I want to share the single biggest mistake I see people make when buying grow lights.

**The Mistake: Trusting "Wattage Equivalent" Marketing**

You've seen the claims:
- "2000W Equivalent LED!"
- "Replaces 1000W HPS!"
- "Ultra High Power 3000W!"

Here's the truth: **These numbers are meaningless.**

A light advertised as "2000W equivalent" might only draw 200W of actual power. And there's no standard for what "equivalent" even means.

**What to Look For Instead:**

1. **Actual Power Draw** (true wattage)
   - Look for "actual draw" or "power consumption"
   - This tells you electricity cost and real output

2. **PPFD Numbers** (most important)
   - Photosynthetic Photon Flux Density
   - Should be 400-600+ for flowering plants
   - Should be measured at multiple distances
   - Verify with independent tests (not manufacturer claims)

3. **Coverage Area** (for flowering, not veg)
   - Manufacturers always list vegetative coverage (larger)
   - Flowering coverage is what matters for most plants
   - Usually 25-40% smaller than veg coverage

**Example:**

"2000W LED" that actually draws 200W:
- Real coverage: 2×2 ft for flowering
- Costs $120
- Uses 200W × $0.12/kWh = $0.024/hour

Vs. quality "300W LED" that draws 300W:
- Real coverage: 3×3 ft for flowering
- Costs $250
- Uses 300W × $0.12/kWh = $0.036/hour
- **Worth the extra cost for more coverage and quality**

## Email 3: Story + Lesson (Day 4)

**Subject:** How I got 30% more yield without upgrading my lights

**Preview Text:** It's not about buying better lights - it's about using them right

**Body:**

Hey,

Quick question: Have you set up your lights yet?

If you have, there's a simple trick that can boost your results by 30% or more - **without buying anything new**.

It's all about light height and intensity management.

**The Problem Most People Have:**

They hang their lights at one height and leave them there for the entire grow. Big mistake.

Plants need different light intensities at different stages:

**Seedlings:**
- Low intensity (200-300 PPFD)
- Lights 24-36 inches away
- Why: Fragile, can't handle intense light
- Signs of too much: Bleaching, stunted growth

**Vegetative Growth:**
- Medium intensity (400-600 PPFD)
- Lights 18-24 inches away
- Why: Building leaves and stems
- Signs of too little: Stretching, thin stems

**Flowering/Fruiting:**
- High intensity (600-1000 PPFD)
- Lights 12-18 inches away
- Why: Maximizing photosynthesis for flowers/fruit
- Signs of too much: Leaf tacoing, burnt tips

**The 30% Boost:**

By adjusting your light height as plants grow, you give them exactly what they need at each stage:
- Seedlings don't get fried
- Vegetative growth doesn't stretch
- Flowering gets maximum light without stress

This is why **adjustable hangers are worth every penny.**

Ratchet hangers ($10-15) let you raise and lower lights in seconds. I adjust mine every 1-2 weeks as plants grow.

**How to Do It:**

1. **Start high** (24-36 inches for seedlings)
2. **Watch the plants** (not a timer):
   - Stretching? Lower the light
   - Bleaching or curling? Raise it
   - Growing perfectly? Leave it
3. **Measure PPFD** if possible:
   - Use a PAR meter ($30-300)
   - Or download a smartphone app (less accurate but helps)
4. **Adjust weekly** as plants mature

**Quick Action:**

Check your light height RIGHT NOW.

If your plants are stretching (long stems, thin leaves), lower your light 2-4 inches. Check again in 24 hours.

If your leaves are curling or bleaching, raise your light 2-4 inches.

Small adjustments = big results.

Questions about your specific setup? Reply with:
- Your light model

- Current height

I'll tell you if you need to adjust.
