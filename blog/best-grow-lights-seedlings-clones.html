Best Grow Lights for Seedlings and Clones: Complete 2026 Guide

Updated December 2026 | 12 min read

Starting seeds indoors? Rooting clones? The right grow light makes the difference between leggy, weak seedlings and stocky, healthy plants ready for transplant. This guide covers everything you need to know about lighting for early plant growth.

Key Takeaway

Seedlings and clones need 10-15 DLI (Daily Light Integral) - much less than flowering plants. You don't need expensive high-powered lights. A quality $50-150 LED panel is perfect for most home growers.

Understanding Seedling Light Requirements

Young plants have very different light needs than mature, flowering plants. Understanding these requirements helps you avoid two common mistakes:

  • Too little light - Seedlings stretch toward the light, becoming tall and weak ("leggy")
  • Too much light - Leaves bleach, curl, or develop brown spots (light burn)

DLI Requirements by Growth Stage

Growth Stage Target DLI Hours of Light PPFD Needed
Germination (first week) 5-10 14-16 hrs 100-150 PPFD
Seedling (weeks 2-4) 10-15 16-18 hrs 150-250 PPFD
Clone rooting 8-12 18-24 hrs 100-200 PPFD
Vegetative growth 15-25 18 hrs 250-400 PPFD
Flowering 35-50+ 12 hrs 600-1000+ PPFD

As you can see, seedlings need roughly 1/3 to 1/4 the light intensity of flowering plants. This is great news for your budget - you don't need to spend $300+ on a powerful LED until you're ready to flower.

Types of Grow Lights for Seedlings

Three main types of grow lights work well for seedlings. Each has pros and cons:

1. T5 Fluorescent Lights

T5 tubes have been the industry standard for seedlings for decades. They produce even, diffused light across a large area with minimal heat.

Pros

  • Low upfront cost ($40-80)
  • Very low heat - can place 2-4" from plants
  • Even light spread across wide areas
  • Proven, reliable technology

Cons

  • Higher electricity cost (less efficient than LED)
  • Bulbs need replacing every 12-18 months
  • Not powerful enough for flowering
  • Bulky fixtures

Best T5 for seedlings: The Hydrofarm Jump Start ($45-55) has been the go-to seedling light for years. It covers a standard 10x20 seedling tray perfectly and includes a height-adjustable stand.

2. LED Seedling Panels

LED technology has improved dramatically. Modern LED panels designed for seedlings are now the best choice for most growers.

Pros

  • 40-50% more efficient than fluorescent
  • 50,000+ hour lifespan (no bulb changes)
  • Full spectrum light
  • Some models work for both seedlings AND flowering

Cons

  • Higher upfront cost ($60-150)
  • Must hang higher (18-24" for seedlings)
  • Cheap models have poor quality drivers
  • Point-source light (less even than T5)

3. LED Light Bars (Clone/Seedling Specific)

Some LED fixtures are designed specifically for propagation (seedlings and clones). These are lower-powered but optimized for early growth stages.

Pro Tip: The "Grow Then Upgrade" Strategy

Many experienced growers use a dedicated seedling light ($50-80), then transfer plants under a more powerful LED ($150-400) for vegetative and flowering stages. This is more efficient than running a high-powered light at low intensity for seedlings.

Seedling Light Setup: Step by Step

Follow this process for best results:

1

Choose Light Height

Start with LED lights 24 inches above seedlings. T5 fluorescents can be 4-6 inches away. Adjust based on plant response.

2

Set Timer for 16 Hours

16 hours on / 8 hours off works for most seedlings. Cannabis clones often do better at 18/6 or even 24/0.

3

Watch for Stretching

If seedlings become "leggy" (tall, thin stems), move the light closer or increase duration. This is the most common seedling problem.

4

Watch for Light Stress

Bleached leaves, brown spots, or curling edges mean too much light. Raise the light or dim if possible.

5

Gradually Increase Intensity

As seedlings grow true leaves (not cotyledons), gradually lower the light or increase dimmer settings over 1-2 weeks.

Clone-Specific Lighting Tips

Clones have unique lighting needs compared to seedlings:

  • Lower intensity needed - Clones don't have roots yet, so they can't process as much light. Keep intensity 25-50% lower than seedlings.
  • Higher humidity matters more - Without roots, clones absorb water through leaves. High humidity (70-80%) reduces stress.
  • 18-24 hours of light - Many growers use 24/0 for clones to prevent stress from darkness.
  • Cool light preferred - T5 or LED at 18-24" keeps dome temperatures stable.

Clone Dome Warning

Never place a powerful LED too close to a humidity dome. Heat buildup can cook clones. Keep LEDs at least 24" above dome lids, or use a T5/low-power LED designed for propagation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Using Household Bulbs

Regular LED or CFL bulbs from the hardware store won't work. They lack the proper spectrum (PAR) that plants need. You need purpose-built grow lights.

Mistake #2: Starting with a Powerful Light

A 300W LED is overkill for seedlings. You'll waste electricity and risk light burn. Match light intensity to growth stage.

Mistake #3: Inconsistent Light Schedule

Seedlings need consistent light cycles. Invest in a $10 mechanical timer. Inconsistent lighting causes stress and irregular growth.

Mistake #4: Ignoring Light Distance

The same light at 6" vs 24" delivers wildly different intensity. Always check manufacturer recommendations and adjust based on plant response.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many watts do seedlings need?

Seedlings need 15-30 watts per square foot of LED light, or 30-50 watts per square foot of fluorescent light. A typical 10x20 inch seedling tray (1.4 sq ft) needs 20-40W of LED or 40-70W of fluorescent lighting.

How far should grow lights be from seedlings?

LED grow lights should be 18-24 inches from seedlings. T5 fluorescent lights can be placed 2-4 inches away. Start higher and gradually lower if seedlings become leggy (stretching toward the light).

Can I use any LED light for seedlings?

No. Household LED bulbs lack the proper spectrum for plant growth. Seedlings need lights with wavelengths in the 400-700nm range (PAR). Purpose-built grow lights or full-spectrum horticultural LEDs are necessary.

How long should grow lights be on for seedlings?

Seedlings need 14-18 hours of light daily. Most growers use 16 hours on / 8 hours off. This mimics long summer days and promotes vegetative growth without stressing the plants.

T5 or LED for seedlings - which is better?

Both work well, but LEDs are generally better for most growers in 2026. LEDs use 40-50% less electricity, last 5x longer, and produce less heat. T5s are cheaper upfront but cost more over time. LEDs are the better long-term investment.
