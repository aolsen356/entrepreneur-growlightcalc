Grow Light Watts Per Square Foot: Complete Wattage Guide

Updated: December 2026 | 12 min read

"How many watts do I need?" is the most common question from new indoor growers. This guide breaks down wattage requirements for every light type, plant type, and grow space size so you can size your lighting system correctly.

Quick Answer: LED Watts Per Square Foot

  • Leafy greens & herbs: 20-30 watts per sq ft
  • Vegetables (tomatoes, peppers): 30-40 watts per sq ft
  • Flowering plants (cannabis): 35-50 watts per sq ft
  • Seedlings: 15-25 watts per sq ft

These are for quality LED lights (2.5+ umol/J efficiency). Budget LEDs may need 20-30% more wattage.

Understanding Grow Light Wattage

Wattage measures how much electricity a light consumes, not how much usable light it produces. This is why a 300W LED can outperform a 600W HPS - efficiency matters as much as raw power. For a detailed comparison of these technologies, see our LED vs HPS grow lights guide.

Actual Wattage vs. Marketing Claims

Many budget lights use misleading wattage claims. A "1000W LED" that only draws 200W from the wall is not providing 1000W of light - it's providing 200W worth. Always check actual power draw.

How to Find True Wattage

  • Look for "Actual Power Draw" in specifications
  • Check amperage rating and multiply by voltage (A x V = W)
  • Use a Kill-A-Watt meter to measure real consumption
  • Ignore "Replacement" or "Equivalent" claims entirely

Watts Per Square Foot by Plant Type

Leafy Greens & Herbs: 20-30 W/sqft

Lettuce, spinach, kale, basil, and cilantro need moderate light. They don't flower/fruit, so lower intensity works well. High light can actually make lettuce bitter.

  • Target PPFD: 200-400 umol/m2/s
  • Target DLI: 12-17 mol/m2/d
  • Example: 2x4 (8 sqft) = 160-240W LED

Fruiting Vegetables: 30-40 W/sqft

Tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, and strawberries need more light to produce fruit. Flowering and fruit development are energy-intensive processes. Combine proper wattage with optimal light schedules for maximum yields.

  • Target PPFD: 400-600 umol/m2/s
  • Target DLI: 20-30 mol/m2/d
  • Example: 4x4 (16 sqft) = 480-640W LED

Cannabis (Flowering): 35-50 W/sqft

Cannabis is a high-light plant, especially during flowering. Maximum light drives maximum bud development. With CO2 supplementation, even higher wattages are beneficial.

  • Target PPFD: 600-1000 umol/m2/s (up to 1500 with CO2)
  • Target DLI: 40-65 mol/m2/d
  • Example: 4x4 (16 sqft) = 560-800W LED

Seedlings & Clones: 15-25 W/sqft

Young plants have limited capacity for high light. Excessive intensity causes stress and slows development. Start low and increase as plants mature. See our best grow lights for seedlings guide for specific recommendations.

  • Target PPFD: 150-300 umol/m2/s
  • Target DLI: 8-15 mol/m2/d
  • Example: 2x2 (4 sqft) = 60-100W LED

Why Efficiency Matters More Than Wattage

Two 300W lights can produce vastly different amounts of usable light. Efficiency (measured in umol/J) tells you how much light you get per watt. Learn more about measuring light output in our DLI guide:

Same Wattage, Different Output

Budget LED (1.8 umol/J) 300W = 540 umol/s total output
Quality LED (2.5 umol/J) 300W = 750 umol/s total output
Premium LED (2.9 umol/J) 300W = 870 umol/s total output

The premium light produces 61% more usable light from the same electricity!

How to Calculate Your Wattage Needs

Step 1: Calculate Your Grow Area

Length (ft) x Width (ft) = Square Feet

Example: 4ft x 4ft = 16 square feet

Step 2: Determine Watts Per Square Foot

Based on your plant type and light quality:

  • Leafy greens: 25 W/sqft
  • Vegetables: 35 W/sqft
  • Flowering plants: 40-50 W/sqft

Step 3: Calculate Total Wattage

Square Feet x Watts Per Sq Ft = Total Wattage Needed

Example: 16 sqft x 40 W/sqft = 640W for flowering

Common Wattage Mistakes

Mistake 1: Trusting "Equivalent" Wattage

A "1000W equivalent" light drawing 200W provides 200W of light, period. Always check actual power draw.

Mistake 2: Using HPS Wattage Rules for LED

Old rules like "50W per sqft" were for HPS. Modern full spectrum LEDs are more efficient - 35-40W/sqft of quality LED equals 50-60W/sqft of HPS.

Mistake 3: Ignoring Light Distribution

A single 600W light won't cover a 5x5 as well as two 300W lights spread out. Consider coverage, not just total wattage.

Mistake 4: Oversizing for Small Spaces

A 600W light in a 2x2 will bleach your plants. Match wattage to space - you can always dim down if needed.

Key Takeaways

  • Quality LED: 35-50 watts per square foot for flowering
  • Always check actual power draw, not marketing claims
  • Efficiency (umol/J) matters as much as raw wattage
  • Match wattage to your specific plant type and growth stage
  • Consider both coverage and total output when choosing lights
  • Higher wattage = higher electricity costs - factor this into your budget
