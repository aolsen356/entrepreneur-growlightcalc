Growing Microgreens Under Grow Lights: Complete 2026 Guide

Want fresh, nutrient-packed microgreens year-round? LED grow lights make it easy. Here's exactly what you need to know about lighting, DLI requirements, and the best budget-friendly setups for microgreens.

Published: December 30, 2025 • 9 min read

Quick Takeaway

  • Microgreens need 12-16 DLI (Daily Light Integral) - less than fruiting plants but more than leafy greens
  • Budget: T5 fluorescent ($30-60) or basic LED panels ($40-80) work perfectly for 1-2 trays
  • Run lights 16-18 hours/day for fastest growth (7-14 days from seed to harvest)
  • Position lights 6-12 inches above trays - closer = more intense light but higher heat

Why Grow Microgreens Indoors?

Microgreens are the perfect indoor crop for beginners and experienced growers alike. Unlike full-size vegetables that take months, microgreens go from seed to harvest in just 7-14 days. They're nutrient-dense (up to 40x more vitamins than mature plants), sell for $25-50 per pound at farmers markets, and require minimal space.

The catch? Natural sunlight isn't reliable year-round, especially in winter. A south-facing window provides maybe 4-8 DLI in January - not enough for healthy microgreens. This is where grow lights become essential, not optional.

Why 2026 Is the Year to Start Growing Microgreens

The microgreens market is projected to grow 12.3% annually through 2030. Restaurants pay premium prices ($30-40/lb) for locally-grown, pesticide-free microgreens. A 2-tray home setup can produce $200-400/month in profit while costing just $15-20 in electricity and seeds. Plus, LED grow light prices have dropped 40% since 2022 - entry costs are lower than ever.

Understanding DLI for Microgreens

DLI (Daily Light Integral) measures the total amount of light your plants receive in a 24-hour period. It's measured in mol/m²/day. Think of it like watering - plants need a certain "dose" of light per day, just like they need a certain amount of water.

DLI Requirements by Crop Type

Crop Type Target DLI Example Varieties
Low-Light Microgreens 10-12 DLI Peas, wheatgrass, cilantro
Medium-Light Microgreens 12-16 DLI Radish, broccoli, kale, arugula
High-Light Microgreens 16-20 DLI Sunflower, basil, mustard
For comparison: Tomatoes/peppers need 25-35 DLI, lettuce needs 12-14 DLI

The good news? Microgreens need significantly less light than fruiting plants like tomatoes or cannabis. This means you can use smaller, cheaper lights and still get excellent results.

Best Grow Lights for Microgreens in 2026

You don't need expensive high-wattage lights for microgreens. Since they're harvested young and don't need to flower or fruit, simple LED panels or T5 fluorescent fixtures work perfectly. Here's what to look for:

Key Features for Microgreens Lights

  • Full spectrum or white light: Microgreens grow well under any full-spectrum LED. Skip the purple "blurple" lights - white LEDs work better and don't hurt your eyes.
  • Coverage area: Standard 10x20 trays need about 2 square feet of light coverage. Look for fixtures rated for 2-4 sq ft.
  • Adjustable height: You'll need to raise lights as microgreens grow. Fixtures with chains or adjustable hangers are ideal.
  • Low heat output: LEDs run cool, which is important for microgreens that can be damaged by excess heat.
  • Energy efficiency: Microgreens lights run 16-18 hours/day. A 45W LED panel costs about $3-4/month in electricity vs $8-10/month for equivalent T5 fluorescent.

Complete Microgreens Grow Light Setup Guide

Having the right light is just the first step. Here's how to set up your microgreens operation for maximum yield and efficiency.

Step 1: Choose Your Growing Space

Microgreens grow well in almost any indoor space with access to electricity. Popular options:

  • Wire shelving units: A 4-tier 48"x18" shelving unit from Home Depot ($50-70) can hold 12-16 trays under lights. Each shelf gets its own light fixture.
  • Countertop/table: Start small with 2-4 trays on a kitchen counter or spare table. Hang lights from above or use adjustable stands.
  • Basement or garage: Year-round controlled environment, no sunlight needed. Just need stable 65-75°F temps.

Step 2: Position Lights at the Right Height

Light intensity follows the inverse square law - doubling the distance quarters the light intensity. For microgreens:

Optimal Light Heights by Growth Stage:

Days 1-3 (Germination)
Keep trays covered and dark. No light needed until seedlings emerge.
Days 3-7 (Early Growth)
Position lights 6-10 inches above trays. Seedlings are stretching, need moderate light.
Days 7-14 (Harvest Stage)
Position lights 10-14 inches above. Prevent stretching, maximize color development.

Pro Tip: If microgreens are stretching tall and thin (etiolated), lower your lights or increase the photoperiod. If leaves are curling or bleaching, raise the lights 2-4 inches.

Step 3: Set Your Light Timer

Consistency is key. Use a basic 24-hour timer ($8-12) to automate your light schedule. Recommended photoperiods:

Light Schedule DLI Delivered Best For
12 hours on / 12 off 10-12 DLI (low) Peas, wheatgrass, cilantro
16 hours on / 8 off 14-16 DLI (ideal) Most microgreens (recommended)
18 hours on / 6 off 16-20 DLI (high) Sunflower, basil, mustard
24 hours on (continuous) 20-25+ DLI (very high) Not recommended - plants need dark periods for rest

Most growers use 16/8: Lights on 7am-11pm. This delivers enough DLI for fast growth while giving plants a dark rest period for optimal metabolism.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Not Enough Light

Symptoms: Tall, spindly, pale microgreens that fall over. Fix: Lower lights to 6-8 inches, increase photoperiod to 18 hours, or add a second light fixture.

Mistake #2: Overwatering Under Lights

Symptoms: Mold, damping off, stem rot at soil line. Fix: Water from bottom only (pour water in tray, let soil wick up moisture). Increase airflow with a small fan.

Mistake #3: Lights Too Close = Heat Stress

Symptoms: Curled, crispy leaves with brown edges. Fix: Raise lights to 12-14 inches. LED panels can still generate heat at close distances, especially 100W+ models.

Mistake #4: Inconsistent Light Schedule

Symptoms: Uneven growth, some trays ready before others. Fix: Use a timer. Manual on/off creates irregular photoperiods that stress plants and reduce yield consistency.

Best Microgreens Varieties for Grow Lights

Not all microgreens perform equally under artificial light. These varieties thrive under LED/fluorescent grow lights:

Easiest for Beginners:

  • Radish: Fast (6-8 days), forgiving, spicy flavor
  • Broccoli: Hardy, mild flavor, high demand
  • Peas: Sweet, crunchy, low light needs
  • Sunflower: Nutty, substantial, sells well

High-Value Commercial Varieties:

  • Basil: $35-50/lb, restaurants love it
  • Arugula: Peppery, unique, premium pricing
  • Mustard mix: Colorful, spicy, eye-catching
  • Amaranth: Vibrant red/purple, Instagram-worthy

Final Thoughts: Start Growing Today

Microgreens are the perfect gateway into indoor growing under lights. Unlike cannabis or fruiting plants that require months of care, you'll harvest your first crop in 7-14 days. The instant gratification keeps you motivated while you learn the fundamentals of DLI, photoperiod, and light management.

You don't need expensive lights to start. A $40 Barrina T5 4-pack will grow excellent microgreens for personal use or small-scale sales. Once you master the basics, you can upgrade to more powerful lights and scale into a profitable side business.

The key is to start simple: pick 3-4 easy varieties (radish, broccoli, peas, sunflower), set up your lights on a timer, and grow your first few trays. You'll be amazed at what's possible with the right lighting.
