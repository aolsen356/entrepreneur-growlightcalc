How Much Wattage for a 4x4 Tent?

A 4x4 tent has 16 square feet of grow space. The wattage you need depends on what you're growing:

Plant Type Watts/sqft Total for 4x4
Leafy greens, herbs 20-30W 320-480W
Tomatoes, peppers 30-40W 480-640W
Cannabis (veg) 25-35W 400-560W
Cannabis (flower) 35-50W 560-800W

For maximum flowering yields, we recommend 450-600W of quality LED for a 4x4. This provides 28-37W per square foot with efficient diodes. For a deeper dive into wattage requirements, check out our complete watts per square foot guide. Higher wattage (600-800W) is only beneficial with supplemental CO2.

What About "1000W Equivalent" Claims?

Ignore them. Many budget lights claim "1000W equivalent" while only drawing 100-200W. Look at actual wall wattage, not marketing claims. A quality 450W LED outperforms a cheap "1500W equivalent" every time.

What to Look for in a 4x4 Grow Light

1. Actual Wattage (Not Advertised)

Always check the actual power draw, not marketing claims. For a 4x4, you want 400-600W of quality LED. Anything advertising "1000W" while drawing 150W is using misleading marketing.

2. Diode Quality

Samsung LM301B and LM301H are the industry standards. Osram and Bridgelux are also quality options. Avoid lights that don't specify their diode brand - they're likely using inferior chips.

3. Efficiency (umol/J)

Efficiency tells you how much light you get per watt of electricity. Higher is better:

  • 2.5+ umol/J: Good efficiency
  • 2.7+ umol/J: Excellent efficiency
  • 2.9+ umol/J: Premium efficiency

4. Driver Quality

The driver powers the LEDs. MeanWell drivers are the gold standard - reliable and efficient. Inventronics is another quality brand. Cheap drivers fail first and can damage your diodes.

5. PPFD Coverage Map

Quality manufacturers publish PPFD maps showing light intensity across their coverage area. Look for even distribution with minimal drop-off at edges. Center should hit 800-1000+ PPFD at recommended height. To understand how PPFD relates to Daily Light Integral, see our complete DLI guide.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use one 300W light for a 4x4?

For vegetative growth or low-light plants, maybe. For flowering, no - you'll have weak edges and poor yields. Use at least 400W for flowering a full 4x4.

LED or HPS for a 4x4?

LED. A 450W LED produces similar light to a 600W HPS but uses less electricity, produces less heat, and lasts 5x longer. See our LED vs HPS comparison.

How high should I hang my light?

Varies by light intensity: Seedlings 24-36", vegetative 18-24", flowering 12-18". For a complete breakdown, see our grow light distance and height guide.

Is one big light or multiple smaller lights better?

Multiple lights can provide more even coverage and redundancy. However, a single quality light is simpler to manage. For 4x4, either works - two 200-250W lights or one 400-500W light.
