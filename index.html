Understanding DLI (Daily Light Integral)

DLI (Daily Light Integral) measures the total amount of photosynthetically active radiation (PAR) that plants receive during a 24-hour period. It's expressed in moles of light per square meter per day (mol/m2/d).

DLI Requirements by Plant Type

Plant Type Minimum DLI Optimal DLI
Leafy Greens 12 mol/m2/d 17-20 mol/m2/d
Fruiting Plants 20 mol/m2/d 30-40 mol/m2/d
Flowering (Cannabis) 25 mol/m2/d 40-65 mol/m2/d
Seedlings 6 mol/m2/d 10-15 mol/m2/d
Houseplants 4 mol/m2/d 8-12 mol/m2/d

How to Calculate DLI

The formula for calculating DLI is:

DLI = PPFD x (Hours of Light x 3600) / 1,000,000

Where PPFD is measured in micromoles per square meter per second (umol/m2/s). Our calculator handles all these conversions automatically to give you accurate recommendations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many watts per square foot do I need?

For LED lights: 25-50 watts per square foot depending on plant type. Flowering plants need more (40-50W/sqft), while leafy greens need less (25-35W/sqft). HPS lights require more wattage due to lower efficiency.

How far should my grow light be from plants?

This varies by light intensity. Generally: seedlings 24-36 inches, vegetative 18-24 inches, flowering 12-18 inches. Always check your specific light's recommendations and watch for light stress (bleaching, curling).

LED vs HPS - which is better?

LED is more energy-efficient (uses 40% less electricity for same output), produces less heat, and lasts longer (50,000+ hours vs 10,000). HPS has higher initial intensity but higher running costs. For most growers, LED is the better long-term investment.

What is PPFD and why does it matter?

PPFD (Photosynthetic Photon Flux Density) measures light intensity in umol/m2/s. It tells you how much usable light reaches your plants. Higher PPFD = more photosynthesis = faster growth. Most flowering plants want 600-1000 PPFD.
